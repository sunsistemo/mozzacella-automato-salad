Generating Random Numbers with Cellular Automata
By Martijn van Beest, Jaro Camphuijsen & Rahiel Kasim

Why?

Random numbers are an essential ingredient of all computer programs involving probabilities. They are used in stochastic simulations like the Monte-Carlo method, to implement randomized algorithms and even in cryptography to provide secure communication online with for example your bank. Dysfunctioning random-number generators (RNG) lead to spurious results: they can invalidate the statistics of your experiment by introducing a bias, or destroy the security guarantees of cryptography. It is thus of utmost importance that the random numbers in use are actually random.
